The Wisconsin Emerging Scholars (WES) is a program where calculus students attend small, seminar-style discussion sections. The WES sections meet for more hours per week than a normal calculus discussion section, and they are designed to challenge the students, giving them more conceptual tasks and problems.

In college, my favorite courses were seminars, where I got to ask many questions and was pushed to think harder. WES is the closest thing to a seminar in math, and I love to teach WES sections!

With the WES section, you get two extra math credits. You also get to interact with me a lot more than a regular calculus student would; therefore, you can more easily ask for a recommendation letter in the future. For more information, please refer to our department's page for the WES program or send me an email.
